## 🧾 Executive Summary
This project analyzes heart disease risk among 3000 patients, where approximately 67% were affected. The main objective was to identify key factors contributing to heart disease and provide actionable insights. Interactive dashboards were developed to analyze medical conditions, lifestyle habits, and demographic factors. The analysis revealed that high blood pressure, cholesterol, diabetes, stress, and obesity are the major drivers of heart disease. These insights help healthcare providers identify high-risk patients early, improve preventive care, and make data-driven decisions. Future improvements include building predictive models and integrating real-time health data for enhanced analysis
- 67% patients are affected by heart disease
- Higher risk is associated with high BP, high cholesterol, diabetes, and high stress
- Overweight and obese individuals show significantly higher disease counts
- Low physical activity and unhealthy habits (smoking/alcohol) contribute to increased risk
- Male patients show slightly higher vulnerability compared to females

## 📌Methodology
- Data Understanding :
    - Explored patient data including BP, cholesterol, diabetes, stress, habits, gender
- Data Cleaning & Transformation :
    - Used Excel / Power Query
    - Handled missing values (“Not Mentioned”)
    - Standardized categories (BP levels, stress levels, weight groups)
- Feature Engineering :
    - Created segments:
    - Weight categories (Overweight, Obese, etc.) based on standard BMI 
    - Risk levels (Cholesterol, Stress)
    - Lifestyle indicators (Smoking, Alcohol)
- Data Analysis :
    - Compared Heart Disease vs Healthy patients
    - Performed segmentation analysis:
        - By gender
        - By lifestyle
        - By medical conditions
- Visualization :
    - Built 3 dashboards:
          - Overview of CHD
          - Gender-based analysis
          - Hierarchical drill-down analysis
